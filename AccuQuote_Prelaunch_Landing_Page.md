## 1. The Strategic Frame

This isn't a "Coming Soon" page. That's passive. It's **the foundation of the launch community**. Every email captured today is a vote of "yes, this pain is real" + a future tester + a future referrer.

The page has to do four things simultaneously:

1. **Stop the scroll** (pain hook, same logic as the ads)
2. **Earn trust without product** (founder story, "built with tradies" community angle)
3. **Give a reason to sign up TODAY** (founding member, limited spots, lifetime price lock)
4. **Capture the bare minimum to follow up** (email + trade type; nothing else)

The big mental shift: **don't sell the app. Sell *joining the build*.** Tradies have seen 50 "Coming Soon" pages. Few have seen "Help us build it — and get lifetime pricing for it."

### Section 2 — The Founder Story

**Why this section is non-negotiable:** Tradies trust people, not brands. The founder story is the single biggest trust-builder when the product doesn't exist yet. Use this verbatim:

---

**I'm Luke. I run a digital agency in Watford — [Slick Digital](https://slickdigital.co.uk). For two years now, I've been building websites for UK tradesmen. Plumbers, sparkies, builders, plasterers, roofers.**

That's a lot of conversations with a lot of tradies. Different trades, different towns. Same recurring problem.

**Quoting was killing them.**

Not the work itself — they love the work. The *quoting*. Sundays at the kitchen table. Two hours per quote. Materials prices that had moved 40% since they last updated their template. Customers ghosting after a Sunday lost to a proposal. Losing jobs to whoever quoted first.

And every time materials went up — which is constantly — the maths got worse. One of my clients put it bluntly: he was doing two days of unpaid work a week just to feed the rest of the work.

**Then I met Gary.**

Gary's a plasterer out of Kent. Twenty years on the tools, sharp as a tack. He'd built himself an absolute Frankenstein of a quoting system — one app to scan the room dimensions, ChatGPT to scope the job, a tool connected to his Travis Perkins account for live materials prices, and a fourth app to spit out a branded PDF.

Four apps. One quote.

But it worked. Sort of. Gary cut his quoting time from hours to minutes. He started getting home at 3pm with the day's quotes already sent. His evenings came back. His weekends came back. He told me he was happier than he'd been in years — transforming spaces during the day, then being a husband and dad in the evening instead of buried in spreadsheets and supplier websites.

The thing is, Gary's the kind of bloke who can stitch four apps together with duct tape. **Most tradies can't, won't, and shouldn't have to.**

That was the moment. I'd watched my clients lose evenings, weekends, family time — to a process that, if Gary's setup was anything to go by, could take five minutes from a single app.

**So I'm building it.**

AccuQuote condenses Gary's whole hack into one tool. Measure the room with your phone camera. AI scopes the job. Live UK supplier prices populate the materials list. A pro PDF proposal lands in your customer's WhatsApp before you've left the driveway. Deposits, reminders, follow-ups — handled.

I'm building it with 200 founding tradies who've signed up to help shape it before launch. Lifetime pricing locked at half the public rate. Names in the in-app credits. Direct line to me while we build.

If you're a UK tradesman losing weekends to quoting — like Gary used to, like every one of my clients used to — I'd love you to be one of them.

Be one of the first 200.

## 3. The Tech Stack (Render-Based)

Your existing Render setup. Here's the optimal configuration:

### Hosting: Render Static Site (free)
- Free static site hosting
- Auto-deploys from GitHub on push to main
- Custom domain support (point DNS at Render)
- HTTPS auto-provisioned via Let's Encrypt
- Global CDN — fast loading even on patchy 4G
- No charge for the LP itself; only pay when you add backend services

### Site framework: Astro (recommended) or plain HTML+Tailwind
- **Astro** if you want component-based structure, easy to extend later (calculator page, blog posts, etc.). Build command: `npm run build`, publish dir: `dist`
- **Plain HTML + Tailwind** if you want absolute simplicity and zero build complexity. Just upload and serve.

Both deploy to Render cleanly. Astro is the more maintainable long-term play.

### Email + Waitlist: Beehiiv (free up to 2,500 subscribers)
- Embed signup form directly on page (their embed code handles it)
- Built-in waitlist + position tracking (e.g., "you're #47")
- Built-in referral mechanic (the founding-200 share-to-jump-the-queue mechanic)
- Built-in newsletter capability for your monthly build-in-public updates
- Free until you hit 2,500 subs — easily covers Year 1

Alternative if Beehiiv doesn't fit: Resend + custom DB on Render (more flexible, more work).

### Domain: Cloudflare Registrar
- At-cost domain pricing (~£8-12/year)
- Set DNS to Render
- Suggested: accuquote.app or accuquote.co.uk

### Analytics: Plausible (~£8/month) or Cloudflare Analytics (free)
- No cookies = no consent banner
- GDPR-friendly
- Track conversion rate (visitor → signup) from Day 1

### Total Year 1 cost
- Render Static Site: **free**
- Beehiiv: **free** (until 2,500 subs)
- Domain: **~£10/year**
- Plausible: **~£96/year** (or free with Cloudflare Analytics)
- **Total: £10–£110/year**

## 5. The Lead Magnet (Make This in Week 2)

A free tool extends the landing page from "give us your email" to "use this useful thing AND give us your email."

### Recommended: "The Sunday Quote Calculator"

A simple calculator that takes 30 seconds to use:

**Inputs:**
- How many quotes do you send per month?
- Average hours per quote?
- Average quote value (£)?
- Win rate (%)?

**Outputs:**
- Hours per month spent quoting
- Hours per month spent on lost quotes (unpaid)
- £ value of unpaid quote time per year (at their hourly rate)
- Sundays per year lost to quoting

**The kicker:** results page emails them the breakdown + adds them to the waitlist with a tag of "calculator user" — these convert better because they've already engaged.

### Why this works specifically
- It's useful in its own right (good for SEO, sharing in Facebook groups)
- The output number is *always* shocking (typically £8k–£20k/year of unpaid quote time)
- Tradies share shocking numbers
- It builds the email list parallel to the main landing page

### Build it on the same stack
- New page at `/calculator` in your Astro project
- Pure client-side JS for the maths (no backend needed)
- Results submit via Beehiiv form (tag: "calculator")
- Same Render deployment, no extra cost

## 6. How to Drive Traffic Before Launch (£1k Budget)

### Channel 1: LinkedIn (free, immediate)
Personal network post. Tuesday or Wednesday morning. Tag 5-10 trade contacts directly. Expect 20-50 signups from a single post with an existing network.

### Channel 2: Trade Facebook groups (free, slow burn)
Join 8-10 groups. Add value for Weeks 1-2 (no pitching). Week 3, post the founding member offer in ONE group. Check admin rules first.

### Channel 3: Meta ads (£5/day — existing strategy)
Run the 10-ad set to the LP. Pain hooks already built. Lead with Ad 1 (materials) + Ad 2 (Sundays) + Ad 3 (anti-cold-call). Ad copy must match the LP message: "join the founding 200" is the offer, not "try free."

### Channel 4: WhatsApp (your own contacts)
30-50 personal messages to existing tradie contacts. Expect 30-50% response rate.

### Channel 5: Reddit
r/UKTradesmen, r/SelfEmployedUK. Be careful — answer questions thoughtfully for 2-3 weeks before mentioning AccuQuote.

### Channel 6: Build-in-public on X/Twitter
Daily posts about the build. Indie hacker community is supportive.

## 8. What NOT to Do

**Don't fake screenshots of features that don't exist.** Mockups labelled "concept" are fine. AI-generated "real" screenshots are a trust-killer if discovered.

**Don't promise specific launch dates you can't hit.** "Q3 2026" is a target. "Launching July 15" is a commitment. Always under-promise.

**Don't collect more data than email + trade.** Postcode, business name, phone, "how many staff" — none of it. Get email today; survey them properly post-signup.

**Don't run ads to a page that's not optimised yet.** Get 20 organic signups first to test messaging. Then add paid.

**Don't add a "Book a demo" button. Ever. Even now.** Your anti-cold-call positioning is a contract. Keep it.

**Don't take money before you're sure you'll ship.** Founding spots are free until launch. No pre-payments.

**Don't ignore the replies.** Reply personally for the first 100 signups.

## 9. The 30-Day Pre-Launch Roadmap

**Week 1:** Build the page on Render. 10-25 signups from network + LinkedIn.
**Week 2:** Build the calculator at `/calculator`. Launch Meta ads at £5/day. Target: 50 signups.
**Week 3:** Soft-promote in Facebook groups. Community wall fills. Target: 100 signups.
**Week 4:** Iterate based on data. Build email sequence depth. Target: 150-200 signups.

**Signal you've nailed it:** Reply rate > 10%, referral rate > 15%, organic signups growing week-on-week.
**Signal something's off:** Conversion rate < 3%, reply rate < 5%, all signups from paid. Fix messaging before scaling.

## Deployment to Render

### Initial setup
1. Push code to GitHub repo (private OK)
2. In Render dashboard: New → Static Site
3. Connect GitHub repo
4. Configure:
   - **Branch:** main
   - **Build command:** `npm install && npm run build`
   - **Publish directory:** `dist`
   - **Node version:** 20.x
5. Deploy
6. Add custom domain (accuquote.app or .co.uk)
7. Update DNS at Cloudflare:
   - Add CNAME record pointing to your Render URL
   - Or A records to Render IPs (Render provides these)

### Environment variables (if needed)
- `BEEHIIV_API_KEY` — for server-side form submission (only if not using embed)
- `BEEHIIV_PUBLICATION_ID` — your Beehiiv pub ID

### Auto-deploy
Render auto-deploys on every push to main. No manual steps after initial setup.
