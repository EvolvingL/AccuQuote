Quoting Software for Plumbers: Accurate Prices, Faster Jobs, Happier Customers

AccuQuote Team • 5 February 2025 • 6 min read
Copper pipe is up 35% since 2020. Boiler prices have moved by £150+ in six months. If your last quote was based on prices you remember rather than prices you checked, you absorbed that difference — and you didn't have to.

Plumbing quotes are tricky in a very specific way: the job you can see is rarely the whole job. You survey a bathroom, price the suite and the tiling and the waste fittings — and on day one you find the old pipework is 1/2" imperial copper that doesn't mate cleanly to modern compression fittings. Or the soil pipe is blocked and needs rodding before the new WC goes in. Or the customer assumed you'd replace the section of floor where the old bath has been dripping for three years.

Managing this uncertainty is part of being a good plumber. But it shouldn't mean you're quoting from rough memory and hoping for the best. AccuQuote was built with over 200 UK tradesmen who were all losing money the same way — and this guide covers how to stop.

The Unique Challenges of Plumbing Quotes

Variable pipe routes

Where does the soil pipe actually run? How far is the boiler from the hot water cylinder? Is the heating system a combi, a system, or an old gravity-fed S-plan with a tank in the loft? These things determine how much pipe you need and how hard the job is, and you often can't tell without opening some walls. Plumbing quotes need to reflect this uncertainty honestly rather than ignoring it and arguing about it later.

Material cost volatility

Copper pipe, push-fit fittings, radiators, valves — prices from Plumb Center, Toolstation and Screwfix all shift regularly. A boiler price can move by £150 in six months. If your quote was based on a price you saw three months ago, you're either absorbing the difference or explaining yourself to the customer.

Job type complexity

A bathroom fit-out and a boiler swap and an emergency leak repair are three completely different jobs. They require different materials, different tools, different skill sets and different time estimates. Generic quoting software that just gives you a blank form isn't much help. You need templates that understand what a typical bathroom refit involves versus a combi boiler swap versus a radiator addition.

Gas Safe certification costs

If you're Gas Safe registered and doing boiler work, the certification cost and any commissioning fees need to be in the quote. They often aren't, because they feel like overhead — but they're not overhead, they're a direct cost of that specific job.

The forgotten cost: A Benchmark commissioning document and boiler registration currently costs around £55–75 in time and fees. A Landlord Gas Safety Record (CP12) is around £65–90 to produce. Neither typically makes it into a rough quote. Both absolutely should.

What Plumbing Quoting Software Should Handle

To be genuinely useful for a plumber, quoting software needs to:

  • Know common job types. Bathroom suite supply and fit, boiler swap, new radiator, heating system flush, leak investigation and repair — each has a typical bill of materials and labour estimate that you refine rather than building from scratch.
  • Price materials from real suppliers. Screwfix, Toolstation, Plumb Center, City Plumbing — current prices, not placeholder figures. A Grohe shower valve costs very differently depending on range. The quote needs to reflect what you'll actually spend.
  • Handle contingency for hidden work. Add a clearly-labelled contingency for "investigation/access" rather than quietly padding the margin. Customers who can see a contingency line understand why it's there — customers who discover the "hidden" markup don't.
  • Include Gas Safe and certification costs. As standard line items, not afterthoughts.
  • Work quickly on site. You can't spend 45 minutes in someone's bathroom typing up a quote on a phone. The tool needs to be fast — scan, describe, review, send.

FAQs About Quoting Plumbing Work

How do I price a boiler swap in the UK?

A standard combi boiler swap (like-for-like position, no flue extension, existing pipework in good condition) typically runs £1,800–2,800 all-in including the boiler, labour, new controls, commissioning and Gas Safe documentation. Worcester Bosch and Viessmann are at the premium end; Vaillant and Ideal sit in the mid-range. Always get merchant pricing on the boiler before quoting — retail prices and trade prices differ significantly at Plumb Center or City Plumbing.

Should I charge a call-out fee for leak investigations?

Yes. A leak investigation is a diagnostic job — you're spending time and expertise identifying the source, not just fixing it. A £75–120 investigation fee, credited back against the repair quote if the customer proceeds, is standard practice and protects you from spending two hours on a job that turns out to be a hairline crack behind a tile.

How do I handle plumbing jobs where the price depends on what you find?

Clearly scope what is included and what isn't in your quote, with a contingency line for investigative work. "Quote is based on existing pipework being in good condition. Any replacement of corroded or non-compliant pipework found during works will be charged at £X/hour plus materials." Customers who see this upfront are never surprised.

What's a fair day rate for a plumber in the UK in 2025?

Sole trader plumbers in most of England charge £240–320/day in 2025. London and South East runs £320–420/day. Gas Safe-registered plumbers doing heating and boiler work tend to be at the higher end of these ranges. When quoting labour, be explicit about whether your day rate includes materials markup or whether materials are priced separately at cost.
