Admin is eating the working week

UK small businesses waste an average of 33 hours per month on admin tasks. Field service workers — including tradespeople — spend up to 7 hours every week on low-value administrative tasks instead of billable work.

Sources: Sage Hidden Admin Burden Report, May 2025 · Salesforce Field Service Agentic AI Report, 2025

📄

Quoting is the biggest time sink

A properly written trade estimate — site visit, material calculations, pricing, formatting, sending — takes 30–60 minutes per job. At 3–5 quotes a week, that's up to 4 hours of unpaid admin every single week. Trade software providers report saving users "a full day a week" on paperwork.

Sources: YourTradebase · QuoteSmith Blog, 2026 · MyBuilder community data

💸

Late payments threaten livelihoods

81% of tradespeople are currently owed money, chasing an average of 7 overdue invoices at once. 23% worry they can't cover personal expenses. 14,000 UK businesses close every year — approximately 38 per day — directly because of late payments.

Sources: Direct Line / Opinium, Oct 2024 · GOV.UK Late Payments Research, 2025

🏗

The industry is the last to go digital

70% of UK sole traders are still unaware of Making Tax Digital changes. CITB research found that innovative technology "is generally limited to small pilots or trials." 81% of tradespeople believe AI could help them do their jobs more efficiently — but almost none have access to tools built for them.

Sources: Sage & Checkatrade Parliamentary Event, 2025 · CITB Digital Future Report · Salesforce, 2025

📉

Slow quotes mean lost work

Construction had the longest average payment delays of any sector: 38.2 days. Only 57% of construction firms report invoices paid within agreed terms. Tradespeople who are slow to quote lose jobs to competitors who respond faster — yet most are still doing it by hand.

Sources: Coface 2025 UK Payment Survey · QuickBooks UK Late Payments Report, 2025

Demand is growing faster than supply

The home improvement and repair sector is expected to grow 40% over the next 10 years. The UK needs 1.3 million new tradespeople to meet that demand. The tradespeople who exist need to work smarter, not harder — and quoting is the most fixable bottleneck.

Source: Checkatrade Trade Skills Index, 2024
