How it works
Three steps. Done before the kettle boils.
Step 1
📐

Scan or measure the space

Point your phone at the room and AccuQuote instantly captures the dimensions — length, width, height, the lot. Already got measurements? Type them in instead. Works for standard rooms, L-shapes, extensions, anything.

No tape measure needed. No sketching on the back of an envelope.
Step 2
🔧

Describe the job

Tell AccuQuote what the job involves — in your own words, just like you'd explain it to an apprentice. It turns that into a full list of every material you'll need, with part codes and up-to-date prices from your supplier.

No product codes to look up. No catalogue to flick through.

AccuQuote builds a professional, branded PDF quote — your logo, your labour rate, your margins. Send it to the customer straight from the app. They can pay the deposit online. Job booked. Money in.

Branded to your business. Looks like a proper company sent it.

Questions
Plain answers

Do I need to be good with technology?

No. If you can use WhatsApp, you can use AccuQuote. It's designed for tradespeople, not office workers. No jargon, no complicated setup.

What trades does it work for?

Electricians, plumbers, plasterers, carpenters, decorators — anyone who needs to quote jobs and buy materials. You tell AccuQuote your trade and it adjusts to your way of working.

What if the room shape is unusual?

Draw it. AccuQuote has a custom room tool where you can tap to add corners and walls — L-shapes, T-shapes, extensions, anything. It works out the area for you.

How does it know the price of materials?

AccuQuote links up with trade supplier catalogues to pull current prices. You set your usual supplier once in your profile and it prices everything from there by default.

Can I change the quote before I send it?

Yes — you're always in control. AccuQuote builds the first draft, you review it, tweak anything you like, then send it. Your name, your numbers, your call.

Is there a contract or minimum term?

No contract. Pay monthly, cancel any time. If it's not saving you time, cancel — no questions asked.
